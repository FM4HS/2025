 Eric Topol

Bio: Eric Topol is the Chair of the Department of Translational Medicine and Director and Founder of the Scripps Research Translational Institute. He also serves as Senior Consultant at Scripps Clinic in the Division of Cardiovascular Diseases and holds The Gary and Mary West Chair of Innovative Medicine. Dr. Topol has been elected to the National Academy of Medicine and is among the top 10 most cited researchers in medicine. His principal scientific focus is on harnessing genomic and digital data, together with artificial intelligence, to individualize medicine. In addition to his research and leadership roles, he remains an active, practicing cardiologist.

Bio: Dr. Marzyeh Ghassemi is an Associate Professor at MIT in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) and Institute for Medical Engineering & Science (IMES), and a Vector Institute faculty member holding a Canadian CIFAR AI Chair and Canada Research Chair. She holds MIT affiliations with the Jameel Clinic and CSAIL. Bio: Camille Nebeker is the Director of the UC San Diego Research Ethics Program and a Professor of Public Health, with faculty appointments in the UC San Diego Design Lab and the Herbert Wertheim School of Public Health and Human Longevity Science. In 2018, she co-founded the ReCODE Health center, which advances ethical practices in digital and AI health research—including machine learning and large language models. Xi Zhang
Oura

Title: TBC

Abstract: TBC

Bio: Xi Zhang is the Vice President of Health Sensing at Oura, where he leads a global team of scientists in developing advanced health algorithms for the Oura Ring. Holding a Ph.D. from the University of Michigan, Dr. Zhang's early work included creating the world's first non-invasive thrombolysis robot. His professional journey encompasses roles at Fitbit and Apple, contributing to his extensive expertise in wearable health technology. At Oura, he has been instrumental in pioneering AI-driven features such as the Fertile Window algorithm, enhancing reproductive health tracking for users with diverse cycle patterns . Additionally, Dr. Zhang serves on the editorial board of the journal Ultrasound in Medicine and Biology, focusing on the integration of machine learning and AI in medical applications.
