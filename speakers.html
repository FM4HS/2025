 Eric Topol

Bio: Eric Topol is the Chair of the Department of Translational Medicine and Director and Founder of the Scripps Research Translational Institute. He also serves as Senior Consultant at Scripps Clinic in the Division of Cardiovascular Diseases and holds The Gary and Mary West Chair of Innovative Medicine. Dr. Topol has been elected to the National Academy of Medicine and is among the top 10 most cited researchers in medicine. His principal scientific focus is on harnessing genomic and digital data, together with artificial intelligence, to individualize medicine. In addition to his research and leadership roles, he remains an active, practicing cardiologist.

Bio: Dr. Marzyeh Ghassemi is an Associate Professor at MIT in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS) and Institute for Medical Engineering \& Science (IMES), and a Vector Institute faculty member holding a Canadian CIFAR AI Chair and Canada Research Chair. She holds MIT affiliations with the Jameel Clinic and CSAIL. She leads the “Healthy ML” group at MIT, which focuses on developing and applying machine learning methods to understand and improve health in ways that are robust, private, and fair. She has previously served as a NeurIPS Workshop Co-Chair and General Chair for CHIL.

Bio: Camille Nebeker is the Director of the UC San Diego Research Ethics Program and a Professor of Public Health, with faculty appointments in the UC San Diego Design Lab and the Herbert Wertheim School of Public Health and Human Longevity Science. In 2018, she co-founded the ReCODE Health center, which advances ethical practices in digital and AI health research—including machine learning and large language models.
